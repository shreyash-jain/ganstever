## The strategy in one minute

This 12-month calendar gives Gans-te-Ver a publishable, defensible content engine of 36 posts (3 a month) built on three tracks that ladder a reader from discovery to booking. The Planning track (Top of funnel) is the awareness layer: high-intent "what is there to see at the southern tip" guides — the Agulhas Wine Triangle, the Waenhuiskrans cave, De Hoop, the shipwreck coast, the two-oceans myth — each one a piece a content farm can list but only a thirty-year local family can sequence. The Logistics track (Mid to Bottom of funnel) is the consideration-and-decision layer: the honest drive, when to come, what a whole house costs versus ten hotel rooms, wifi and load-shedding, and exactly how WhatsApp-only booking works — the practical reassurance that turns "interested" into "enquired." The Seasonal track is a twelve-month almanac that closes the loop: a first-person "Gans-te-Ver in [month]" diary that matches publishing to live search demand — whales in winter, wildflowers in spring, the festive house in December, long weekends in autumn — and routes every reader to the WhatsApp CTA at the moment they are dreaming about that month.

The whole calendar leans on the one asset competitors cannot buy: the family has wintered, summered and raised three generations at this exact house since 1991, so every claim is framed as lived knowledge ("the corner of the balcony where the whales hold station," "which petrol stop is the last sensible one," "the gravel is fine for a hatchback"). Awareness posts earn trust through that candour, consideration posts convert it into confidence, and the seasonal almanac keeps the site fresh and seasonally relevant all year. Publishing is sequenced to demand: whale and almanac posts land just before each season, December-booking and festive posts publish in October when families plan Christmas, and long-weekend posts precede the actual 2027 public holidays. The result is a calendar a manager can defend month by month — every post has a reason to exist, a keyword it can win, and a single owner so the 36 reinforce rather than cannibalise each other.
